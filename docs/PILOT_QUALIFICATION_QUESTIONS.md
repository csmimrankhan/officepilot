# Pilot Qualification Questions — OfficePilot AI

Use these questions to determine if a potential pilot user is a good fit for
OfficePilot AI. Score each answer and total at the end. The goal is to identify
firms with measurable pain in invoice processing who value trust, auditability,
and local-first processing.

Administer these questions during a discovery call or via a screening form.
A minimum score of 20 is recommended before proceeding to a demo or pilot
onboarding.

---

## Questions

### 1. How many invoices does your firm/team process per month?

- < 50
- 50–200
- 200–1000
- 1000+

**Score:** 200+ is ideal. Higher volume means more time saved and a stronger
ROI case. Firms processing fewer than 50 invoices per month are unlikely to
see enough benefit to justify the setup effort.

### 2. How do invoices arrive?

- Email (PDF attachments)
- Downloaded from portals
- Paper/scanned
- All of the above

**Score:** Email/PDF is ideal fit. OfficePilot's parser engine is built for
digital PDF invoices. Paper or scanned invoices require OCR and have higher
error rates. Portal downloads are supported but require browser automation
setup.

### 3. Do you currently use Excel or Google Sheets for invoice tracking?

- Yes, heavily
- Sometimes
- No, we use accounting software directly

**Score:** Yes/heavy is ideal. Users who rely on spreadsheets have the most
to gain from automated extraction, validation, and export. They also tend to
feel the pain of manual entry most acutely.

### 4. Do you use QuickBooks Online or Xero?

- QuickBooks Online
- Xero
- Both
- Neither

**Score:** QBO or Xero is a plus. Native accounting sync is a major selling
point. Teams on neither may need manual CSV export workflows, which reduces
the end-to-end automation value.

### 5. How do you currently catch errors in invoice data entry?

- Manual review
- Spot checks
- We don't — mistakes slip through
- Automated validation

**Score:** Manual review or mistakes = high pain. If errors are currently
slipping through, the trust layer (review queue, audit log, warnings) is a
direct solution. Automated validation already in place suggests lower pain.

### 6. How many hours per week does your team spend on invoice data entry?

- < 2 hours
- 2–5 hours
- 5–15 hours
- 15+ hours

**Score:** 5+ hours = high pain. Every hour saved is billable time recovered.
Firms spending 15+ hours per week are top-priority pilots — they feel the
pain daily and will champion the tool internally.

### 7. Does your firm have concerns about cloud AI tools processing client data?

- Yes — we prefer local/on-premise
- Somewhat
- No — we're fine with cloud

**Score:** Local preference = strong fit. OfficePilot is a local-first desktop
app. Data stays on the user's machine. Firms that have rejected cloud-only
solutions are exactly our target audience.

### 8. How important is an audit trail for invoice processing?

- Critical — we need to track every action
- Nice to have
- Not important

**Score:** Critical = strong fit. OfficePilot's audit log captures every
action, approval, and restore. Firms with compliance requirements (audit,
tax, client reporting) are ideal.

### 9. Would approval-gated automation (human reviews before any write/export) be a requirement or a nice-to-have?

- Requirement
- Nice to have
- Not needed

**Score:** Requirement = strong fit. Approval gates are a core differentiator.
Firms that require human-in-the-loop automation trust the system more and
are likelier to adopt.

### 10. Would you be willing to test with sample data first?

- Yes
- No — I want to use my own data

**Score:** Yes = easier onboarding. Sample-data pilots reduce risk, shorten
the sales cycle, and avoid data-privacy concerns during evaluation. A "no"
is not disqualifying but means a slower onboarding process.

### 11. Would a one-click restore of previous invoice versions be valuable?

- Very valuable
- Somewhat
- Not needed

**Score:** Very valuable = strong fit. Version history and one-click restore
signal that the user understands the importance of data safety and wants a
safety net before automating.

### 12. What would make you trust an invoice automation tool? (Select all that apply)

- Open source / source-available code
- Local processing (no cloud)
- Approval gates
- Audit logs
- References from peers

**Score:** Multiple = ideal. The more trust factors resonate, the more
aligned the prospect is with OfficePilot's value proposition. Four or more
selections indicates a near-perfect fit.

## Scoring Guide

Score 0–3 for each answer (3 = best fit), total out of 36.

- **30–36:** Ideal pilot user — high pain, good workflow fit, trust-conscious
- **20–29:** Good pilot user — some pain, likely to benefit
- **10–19:** Possible user — lower pain or workflow mismatch
- **Below 10:** Not ready — educate or pass

### Question Weighting Notes

Questions 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9 carry the most weight in practice because
they directly measure pain level and workflow alignment. A prospect scoring
0 on both volume (Q1) and hours (Q6) is unlikely to benefit regardless of
other answers. Use discretion — a low total score may be offset by strong
trust-factor alignment (Q12) if the prospect has high growth potential.

## Fit Profile Summary

- **Best fit:** Accounting firms processing 200–1000+ invoices/month via
  email/PDF, using Excel and QBO/Xero, with audit/approval/trust concerns.
  Score 30–36.
- **Good fit:** Small businesses processing 50–200 invoices/month, want to
  reduce manual work, open to local-first tools. Score 20–29.
- **Consider:** Teams already fully automated with no pain, or firms
  processing fewer than 50 invoices/month. Score 10–19 — may benefit from a
  lighter tool or later-stage product.
- **Pass:** No pain, cloud-only preference, no audit/approval needs, and
  unwilling to test with sample data. Score below 10.

### Next Steps by Score Range

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 30–36 | Offer immediate pilot onboarding. Schedule demo + kickoff. |
| 20–29 | Schedule a discovery demo. Address any gaps before pilot. |
| 10–19 | Send educational content. Follow up in 3–6 months. |
| Below 10 | Add to newsletter. Do not pursue for active pilot. |

## Interview Notes

Use this section during discovery calls to capture qualitative context that
the scoring questions may miss.

- **Current tools:** What specific software is used today (names, versions)?
- **Pain points:** What is the single most frustrating part of invoice
  processing right now?
- **Decision maker:** Who owns the budget and the decision to adopt new tools?
- **Timeline:** When does the prospect want to start? Is there a busy season
  approaching?
- **IT environment:** Any restrictions on installing desktop software or
  granting file system access?
- **Competing solutions:** Have they tried other tools? Why did those fail?
- **References:** Do they know other firms that might also pilot?

Record notes alongside the score for a complete qualification picture.

## Red Flags

The following answers or behaviors warrant caution regardless of score:

- Unwilling to test with sample data AND unwilling to sign a DPA
- Requires cloud-hosted deployment (no current on-premise option)
- Processing fewer than 20 invoices per month
- Currently using a fully automated solution with no complaints
- Expects the tool to handle handwritten or low-quality scanned invoices
  without human review
- No budget allocated or unclear decision-making authority
- Requests features far outside the current product roadmap (e.g., payroll,
  inventory management)

A single red flag does not disqualify a prospect, but two or more should
trigger a hold decision until clarified.
