# Pilot User Interview Script

A structured script for 30-minute discovery + demo calls with potential pilot users of OfficePilot AI.

---

## 1. Intro (2 min)

"Hi [Name], thanks for your time. I'm building OfficePilot — a local-first invoice automation tool with approval, audit, and restore. I'd love to learn about your current workflow and show you what we're building. This isn't a sales pitch — I genuinely want to understand if this could help and get your feedback."

---

## 2. Pain Discovery (8 min)

Questions:

- "Walk me through what happens when an invoice arrives at your firm."
- "How many people touch each invoice?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of the process?"
- "Have you tried any automation tools before? What didn't work?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal process look like?"

**Listen for:**
- Manual data entry
- Excel hell / spreadsheet fatigue
- Approval gaps
- Audit worries
- Trust concerns with automation
- Lossy handoffs between people
- Duplicate payment risk

---

## 3. Current Workflow (5 min)

- "What software do you use day-to-day for invoices?"
- "How do you track who approved what?"
- "What happens when you need to find an old invoice or version — say from 6 months ago?"
- "How do you handle mistakes — a wrong amount paid, a duplicate, a lost approval?"
- "What does your review/approval chain look like? Who sees it, who signs off?"

---

## 4. Demo (10 min)

Use the demo checklist. Show each step and ask the question below after each one.

**Step 1 — Upload:**
Upload a sample invoice PDF. Point out drag-and-drop and the instant parse feedback.

**Step 2 — Review:**
Show the extracted fields (vendor, amount, date, line items). Highlight the confidence indicators.

**Step 3 — Approve:**
Walk through the approval gate. Show the audit log entry that is created automatically.

**Step 4 — Export:**
Export to Excel. Show the formatted output file.

**Step 5 — Audit Log:**
Navigate to the audit log. Show every event captured: upload, review, approval, export, who did it, when.

**Step 6 — Version History / Restore:**
Show version history for an invoice. Demonstrate restore with the reason-required modal.

**Step 7 — Safety Policy / Kill Switch:**
Show the policy center — permission levels, approval requirements. Show the global kill switch.

**After each step, ask:**
"Does this match how you'd want it to work?"

---

## 5. Reaction Questions (3 min)

- "What surprised you about what you just saw?"
- "What's missing that you'd need before you could use this?"
- "Would you trust this with real client invoices? Why or why not?"
- "What would need to be different for you to use this daily?"

---

## 6. Pricing Sensitivity (2 min)

- "If this saved your team 5 hours per week, what would that be worth to you?"
- "Would you prefer a one-time license or a monthly subscription?"
- "What price point would make you try it without hesitation?"
- "What price point would make you stop considering it?"

---

## 7. Trust / Security Questions (2 min)

- "Does the fact that everything is processed locally on your machine matter to you?"
- "Would open-source or source-available code help you trust the tool more?"
- "Any security or compliance requirements we should know about? SOC2? HIPAA? GDPR?"
- "Would you need a team admin to control who can access the system?"

---

## 8. Objections (3 min)

**Objection: "We already have a system"**

*Response:* "That's fair. What does your current system miss? Things like a full audit trail? Version history? Approval gates with mandatory reasons? File-level restore for mistakes?"

**Objection: "I don't trust AI with invoices"**

*Response:* "That's a common concern. All extraction happens locally on your machine — nothing is sent to the cloud. And you review everything before it's exported. Nothing is automated without your explicit approval."

**Objection: "We use [QuickBooks / Xero / different accounting software]"**

*Response:* "We have QuickBooks and Xero sync available. What do you use? We may be able to add it."

**Objection: "Too busy to try it"**

*Response:* "I understand. Would you be willing to try with sample data? No setup, no integration, no risk — about 5 minutes. If it saves time, great. If not, no pressure."

**Objection: "Not ready yet"**

*Response:* "No problem at all. Would it be OK to check back in 3 months? I can send you a short update on what's new."

**Objection: "I need to run this by [boss / IT / compliance]"**

*Response:* "Absolutely. I can send you a one-page summary with the key security and audit features. Happy to join a call with them too."

---

## 9. Next Steps (2 min)

- "Would you like to try OfficePilot with sample data right now?"
- "May I send you the download link and a 5-minute getting-started guide?"
- "If you try it, would you be open to a 15-minute follow-up call for feedback?"
- "Is there anyone else at your firm who should see this?"
- "Would you like me to send a calendar invite for a follow-up?"

**Record:**
- [ ] Willing to try
- [ ] Sent download link
- [ ] Follow-up call scheduled
- [ ] Referral contact provided

---

## 10. Closing (1 min)

"Thank you so much for your time. I'll send you a summary of what we discussed and the next steps within 24 hours. Your feedback is incredibly valuable — even if this isn't the right time, I appreciate your honesty."
