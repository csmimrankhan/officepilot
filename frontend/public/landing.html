Frequently Asked Questions

What does OfficePilot do?

OfficePilot automates invoice processing. It reads invoice PDFs and emails, extracts line items and totals, lets you review and approve them, then exports to Excel or syncs to QuickBooks/Xero. Everything is logged and restorable.

Is OfficePilot a cloud service?

No. OfficePilot runs entirely on your local machine. All data stays on your computer unless you explicitly connect it to Gmail, QuickBooks, or Xero. No data is sent to external AI providers.

Does OfficePilot require internet access?

The core app works offline. Internet is only needed for optional features: Gmail email import, QuickBooks/Xero accounting sync, and browser automation. If you use only local invoice upload + Excel export, no internet is needed.

Is my data safe?

Yes. OfficePilot is local-first. All invoice data, audit logs, and version history are stored in a local SQLite database. You can export your data at any time. File snapshots before every restore operation. The app has a Safety Policy Center, role-based permissions, and an emergency kill switch.

Can OfficePilot access my bank accounts?

No. OfficePilot does not connect to banks, process payments, or automate banking websites. Banking domains are blocked in browser automation mode. The app is designed for invoice processing — not payment execution.

Does OfficePilot use AI for extraction?

OfficePilot uses a combination of PDF parsing (PyMuPDF, pdfplumber), OCR (Tesseract), and pattern matching to extract invoice data. It does NOT use external AI APIs or cloud LLMs. All extraction happens locally.

Can I use OfficePilot with my accounting software?

OfficePilot supports QuickBooks Online and Xero via their official APIs. Sync is preview-first: you review a draft entry before it reaches your accounting system. Both mock/sandbox and real modes are supported.

Who should use OfficePilot?

OfficePilot is designed for accountants, bookkeepers, administrative teams, small business owners, and BPO/outsourcing firms who process invoices regularly and want a local, auditable, safe automation layer.

Is OfficePilot open source?

OfficePilot is source-available. The code is published on GitHub for review and self-hosting. The project uses a standard open-source license. Commercial use terms are defined in the license.

Does OfficePilot support multiple users?

Yes. OfficePilot has built-in role-based permissions with 5 roles and 18 permissions. The first user to register becomes the owner and can invite/manage other users.

How do I get started?

Download the latest installer from the GitHub releases page, run the setup, and register as the first owner. The built-in onboarding wizard will guide you through whisper model setup, local LLM configuration, and a voice test. Demo mode is also available with sample invoice data.

What is the Voice Recorder?

The Voice Recorder lets you record any workflow by voice. Start recording, perform your steps, stop recording — OfficePilot captures every event and converts it into a reusable skill with AI-generated name and trigger phrases. Replay the skill anytime with approval checkpoints.
